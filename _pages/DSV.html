Deep Support Vectors

Seoul National University
Neurips 2024
Paper Code arXiv

DSVs generated from a deepKKT condition model, trained on ImageNet, CIFAR10, and CIFAR100. Each image is produced without referencing the original training data.

Abstract

Deep learning has achieved tremendous success. However, unlike SVMs, which provide direct decision criteria and can be trained with a small dataset, it still has significant weaknesses due to its requirement for massive datasets during training and the black-box characteristics on decision criteria. This paper addresses these issues by identifying support vectors in deep learning models. To this end, we propose the DeepKKT condition, an adaptation of the traditional Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) condition for deep learning models, and confirm that generated Deep Support Vectors (DSVs) using this condition exhibit properties similar to traditional support vectors. This allows us to apply our method to few-shot dataset distillation problems and alleviate the black-box characteristics of deep learning models. Additionally, we demonstrate that the DeepKKT condition can transform conventional classification models into generative models with high fidelity, particularly as latent generative models using class labels as latent variables. We validate the effectiveness of DSVs using common datasets (ImageNet, CIFAR10 and CIFAR100) on the general architectures (ResNet and ConvNet), proving their practical applicability. (See Fig. 1)

Our goal is to find Deep support vectors in 'any' trained model. As the Support Vectors can 1. Visualize decision boundary 2. Can express the model within few images, corresponding support vectors in deep learning model can 1. unlock the black-box of deep learning model 2. Can distill data from trained model.

In this paper, we propose DeepKKT condition, an adaptation of traditional KKT condition for deep learning models. Using DeepKKT condition, we can find DSVs in 'deployed' models (any trained model with any training dataset)

As support vector itself becomes a model, stationarity condition can be pharaprased as as 'model weights should be constructed solely from support vectors'. The most interesting part of this condition is that when we exploit gradient descent using hinge loss, we get exactly same result as stationarity condition.

Out stationarity condition becomes a generalization of hinge loss.

As DSVs use class labels as latent variables, they can be used to generate images with high fidelity.

Similar to Support Vectors, DSVs also Visualize decision boundary, meaning, we can understand its decision criteria. For example, generated cat DSVs exhibit exaggerated ears. So we can reduce its probability by erasing its ear. Also, only adding three facial dots makes cat into dog.
